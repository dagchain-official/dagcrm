Part A

Getting Started & Login

Open the CRM address in any modern browser (Chrome recommended) and sign in with the email & password provided by your administrator.

  • Dashboard: After login, everyone lands on a dashboard tailored to their role — a founder/admin sees company-wide numbers, a sales person sees their own leads and targets.
  • Left menu: All sections are grouped — Overview, Sales & CRM, Support, HR & People, Finance, Administration. You only see the sections your role is allowed to.
  • Top bar: Global search, notifications (bell), theme toggle, and your profile / settings (gear icon).
  • Settings (gear): Update your name, phone, password, and your personal business email accounts used to send mail to leads.

Part B · Do this first

Initial Setup

Complete these seven sections once, in order, before daily use. They define what your business sells, who your staff are, and how leads flow in. Most live under Administration → Configuration.

1

Businesses

Admin
Administration → Configuration → Businesses

Your separate business units / brands (e.g. FX Artha, DAGChain). Everything — products, leads, revenue, targets — is organised under a business.

What to enter
FieldMeaning
NameBusiness / brand name
DescriptionShort note (optional)
StatusActive / Inactive
Used in → Products, Leads, Revenue, Targets, Integrations, Reports — every business-wise view.
2

Products

Admin
Administration → Configuration → Products

The products / services each business offers. Used on proposals and to link a customer to what they bought.

What to enter
FieldMeaning
BusinessWhich business this product belongs to
NameProduct / service name
Product typeCategory (free text)
PriceStandard price
Revenue typeOne-time / recurring / per-unit / token
Used in → Proposals, Customer products, Revenue.
3

KPI Definitions

Admin
Administration → Configuration → KPI Definitions

Define the performance metrics you want to track — e.g. “Lots Traded”, “Meetings”, “New Deposits”. These drive KPI boards, performance scores, and activity-based incentives.

What to enter
FieldMeaning
NameMetric name
Unitlots, count, ₹, etc.
AggregationHow to total it: sum / count / average / latest
Categorygrowth / activity (used by performance scoring)
SourceManual (typed) or Derived (auto-counted from CRM, e.g. calls/meetings)
Used in → KPI Entries, KPI Board, Performance, Activity Incentives, Traders & Lots.
4

Lead Sources

Admin
Administration → Configuration → Lead Sources

The channels leads come from (Website, Referral, Meta Ads…). Integrations create these automatically; add manual ones here.

Used in → Every Lead carries a source; reports break leads down by source.
5

Users

Admin
Administration → Users

Create a login for every staff member and assign their role & manager. This is the account they sign in with.

What to enter
FieldMeaning
Name / EmailPerson’s name and login email
RoleSuper Admin, Business Head, Sales Manager, Team Leader, Sales Executive, Support, HR, Finance
ManagerWho they report to (builds the org tree)
Phone / StatusContact & active/inactive
Used in → Lead assignment, targets, performance roll-up, payroll (each user also becomes an Employee record for HR).
6

Roles & Permission Matrix

Admin
Administration → Roles & → Permission Matrix

Control exactly what each role can View / Create / Edit / Delete in every module. The Permission Matrix is a simple grid of roles × sections — tick the boxes.

Controls → Which menu items and buttons each person sees. Set this once and it applies everywhere.
7

Integrations

Admin
Administration → Integration Hub

Connect external sources so leads/data flow in automatically. Two kinds:

  • Social ads (per business): Click “Add Integration”, pick a platform (Instagram/Meta, Google Ads, WhatsApp…) and the business. You get a unique webhook link — paste it into that ad account. New leads then arrive tagged to that business and auto-assigned to its team.
  • FXArtha platform: A “Sync” connector that pulls traders, deposits/withdrawals, revenue and lots every minute automatically.
Feeds → Leads, Customers, Revenue, AUM, Contribution and the “Lots Traded” KPI.

Part C · Setup

HR & Payroll Setup

Configure your team structure and the rules that turn performance into pay. Do this once; adjust when policy changes.

8

People — Employees, Departments, Levels

HR / Admin
HR & People → People

Three tabs: Employees (salary, department, level, manager), Departments, and Hierarchy Levels (reporting order — 1 = top).

Key field on Employees
FieldMeaning
SalaryMonthly basic — the single source for CTC & payroll
Hierarchy LevelRank in the org (drives targets & performance)
ManagerReporting manager (used for team roll-ups)
Used in → CTC, Targets, Performance, Payroll.
9

Cost & CTC

HR / Finance
HR & People → Cost & CTC

First define Cost Categories (Laptop, Internet, Visa…), then add each employee’s monthly Employee Costs. Salary + these costs = the employee’s CTC.

Used in → Target = CTC × multiplier; also P&L cost.
10

Target Multipliers & Performance Weights

Admin
HR & People → Rules & Config
  • Target Multipliers: “How many times their cost should each person earn?” e.g. 3× — set globally, per level, or per employee.
  • Performance Weights: How much Revenue / Growth / Activity each count toward the performance score (default 60 / 25 / 15).
Used in → Targets board, Auto Performance, Incentives.
11

Incentive Rules — Slabs, Activity & Formula

HR / Finance
HR & People → Payroll & Incentives and → Rules & Config

Define how incentives are earned. Three tools (use whichever fits):

ToolWhat it does
Incentive SlabsTiers by target-achievement %, e.g. 100–150% → 4% of revenue
Activity IncentivesPer-unit reward on a KPI, e.g. Lots Traded → ₹2 per lot
Formula BuilderCustom rules like “Revenue > Cost × 2 → 10%” without any coding
Per-lot commission is set here — under Activity Incentives, metric = “Lots Traded”, rate = amount per lot. This rate then drives the Traders & Lots report and payroll.
Used in → Incentive Board → Payroll.

Part D · Daily

Daily Sales & CRM

The day-to-day heart of the system — capturing leads, working them, and converting to paying customers.

12

Leads

Sales
Sales & CRM → Leads

Every potential customer. Leads arrive automatically from ads/FXArtha, or you add them manually / import a CSV.

What to enter (manual)
FieldMeaning
Name / Email / PhoneContact details
Country / SourceOrigin & channel
Assigned ToWhich sales person owns it (blank = auto round-robin)
Working a lead (open any lead)
  • Engage buttons — Call, WhatsApp, Email, Send Proposal. Each is logged and the status moves forward automatically (Call → Contacted, Meeting/Proposal → Qualified).
  • Email asks which of your business email accounts to send from.
Flows to → Lead Activities, Opportunities, Proposals, and (on conversion) Customers.
13

Lead Activities

Sales
Sales & CRM → Lead Activities

A log of every call / message / meeting / note across all leads. Mostly created automatically by the Engage buttons; you can also add a note manually.

Used in → Derived KPIs (Calls, Meetings) and each employee’s activity score.
14

Opportunities

Sales
Sales & CRM → Opportunities

A qualified deal in progress. Track the stage (proposal → negotiation → won/lost) and expected revenue. An accepted proposal creates a “won” opportunity automatically.

Used in → Pipeline value on dashboards.
15

Proposals

Sales
Sales & CRM → Proposals (or from a Lead/Customer)

A formal quote with line items. Add products, quantity, price and discount — totals and tax compute automatically. Send it, then mark Accept or Reject.

“Accept” does a lot at once: the lead becomes a Customer, an Opportunity is marked Won, a Revenue entry is created, and the product is marked active — all in one click.
16

Customers (360° view)

Sales / Admin
Sales & CRM → Customers → open a customer

Everything about one customer in one place: revenue, products, tickets, communications, documents, trading activity, and a full timeline.

  • Trading Activity: that customer’s Lots traded, Deposits, Withdrawals, Net AUM (from FXArtha).
  • Reassign RM (admins/managers): hand the customer to another employee — future credit follows the new owner, past stays with the old.
  • Quick actions: add communication, ticket, revenue, proposal, or upload a document.
17

Communications

Sales / Support
Sales & CRM → Communications

A record of messages exchanged with leads & customers (channel, direction, text). Engage actions log here automatically; you can also add entries.

18

Revenue

Finance / Sales
Sales & CRM → Revenue

The single record of money earned. Created automatically by accepted proposals and FXArtha sync; can be added manually.

FieldMeaning
Customer / Business / ProductWho & what
Gross revenueTotal amount
CommissionPartner commission (if any)
Net revenueAuto = Gross − Commission
Used in → P&L, Targets, Performance, Incentives.

Part E

Targets, KPI & Finance

These read your live data and show performance — most are view-only boards, nothing to store.

19

Targets

Managers
Sales & CRM → Targets · assign via Assign Target

Each person’s revenue target (CTC × multiplier) and how much they’ve achieved. Senior roles assign targets to individuals, teams or departments.

20

KPI & Performance

Managers
Sales & CRM → KPI & Performance

Tabs: KPI Board (metric scorecards), Auto Performance, KPI Entries (see Part F), and Performance (weighted 0–100 ranking of your team).

21

Traders & Lots

Managers / Finance
Sales & CRM → Traders & Lots

Per employee, a breakdown of their traders and how many lots each traded (this month & all-time), with estimated per-lot commission. Filter by employee, pick a month, or preview a different rate.

Reads → the “Lots Traded” KPI and your Activity-Incentive rate.
22

Finance — P&L, AUM & Contribution

Finance
Finance → Finance (P&L) · AUM · Contribution
  • P&L: Revenue − Cost = Profit, rolled up the org tree.
  • AUM: deposits − withdrawals = net funds, per employee.
  • Contribution: weighted business contribution from deposits, brokerage, etc.
Note: these boards are calculated live every time you open them — there is nothing to save.

Part F · Monthly

Monthly Payroll & Incentives

Run these at month-end, in order, to pay your team correctly.

23

KPI Entries

HR / Managers
Sales & CRM → KPI & Performance → KPI Entries

The actual metric values per employee. “Lots Traded” fills automatically from FXArtha; other manual KPIs (e.g. Active Traders) are entered here with “+ New”. There’s a Trader column and filter to see per-trader values.

24

Incentive Board

HR / Finance
HR & People → Payroll & Incentives → Incentive Board

Preview each employee’s incentive (slab + activity) for the month. When correct, press “Run” — this writes the incentive into their payroll.

Important: incentives are not paid automatically — you must press Run. Until then, payroll shows no incentive.
25

Payroll

HR / Finance
HR & People → Payroll & Incentives → Payroll

Each employee’s payslip. Enter Bonus and any Deduction; the Final salary = Basic + Incentive + Bonus − Deduction is calculated automatically.

Part G

Recruitment

26

Jobs & Automatic Resume Shortlisting

HR / Admin
HR & People → Recruitment

Post a job, share one link in your ad, and let the system filter resumes for you.

Create a job — enter title, role, required skills (comma-separated), and a shortlist threshold %.
Copy the apply link — each job has its own public link; put it as the “Apply” button in your ad.
Candidate applies — they open the link, fill name/email/phone and upload a resume (PDF). No login needed.
Auto-scoring — the system reads the resume, matches it against the required skills, gives a % match, and auto-shortlists anyone at/above your threshold.
Review — candidates appear ranked by match %, with matched/missing skills and resume download. Mark Shortlist / Hire / Reject.
For resume filtering, use the CRM’s apply link in your ad. A platform’s own lead form only collects name/phone (no resume), so it can’t be scored.

Part H

Support Desk

Tickets

Support
Support → Support Desk

Log and track customer issues. Enter ticket number, category, priority and status; assign to a support agent and add comments. Tickets also show on the customer’s 360° view.
